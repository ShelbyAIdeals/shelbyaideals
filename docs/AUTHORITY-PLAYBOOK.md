# Shelby AI Deals — Authority / Backlink Playbook (Phase 4)

**Created:** 2026-05-24 · Owner: Frank Shelby

## Why this is THE lever

GSC (last 3 months): avg position ~65, 0.61% CTR, **93% of clicks are branded**. Your content is good and gets impressions, but it ranks on page 6 because the **domain has almost no link authority yet**. Phases 1–3 fixed the technical/internal foundation; nothing there moves you to page 1 by itself. **Earned backlinks + brand mentions are what move a young site up the SERP.** This is slow (weeks→months) and mostly off-site, so it can't be automated in code.

> ⚠️ **Never buy links or use automated link schemes.** They violate Google's guidelines and can get a young domain penalized. Everything below is white-hat (earned). Also per house rule: **no self-promotional links on Reddit** (ban risk) — participate for value only.

## Assets you already have (use these as link bait)

- **5 fresh AI coding-agent guides** (Claude Code, Claude Design, Codex, Hermes, OpenClaw) — timely, genuinely useful, low competition. Strongest current linkable assets.
- **17 data-backed comparisons + 8 best-of lists** with per-scenario winners — citable.
- **31 pricing guides** with real plan breakdowns — people link to clear pricing references.
- **Hands-on testing angle** ("7–14 days, real workflows, no sponsored rankings") — credible PR hook.
- **`/llms.txt` + structured data** — helps AI engines cite you (GEO compounds with authority).

## Tactics, prioritized by ROI for a young site

### 1. AI-tool directories & review aggregators (fastest wins)
Submit the site (and individual standout reviews) to directories that list AI-review resources. Targets to pursue:
- Futurepedia, There's An AI For That, Future Tools, AI Tools Directory, TopAI.tools, AIxploria, SaaSHub, AlternativeTo (as a source), Product Hunt (launch the agent-guides collection), BetaList.
- Niche: indie-hacker / solopreneur tool lists, "best AI newsletters/review sites" roundups.
- **Action:** 5 submissions/week. Track approval + live link.

### 2. Tool vendors' "as featured in" / partner pages
Many tools you review positively link back to reviewers and partners.
- For every tool you rated ≥4.0, email their marketing/PR: "We published a hands-on review — happy to share; feel free to quote/link." Provide a pull-quote.
- Apply to affiliate/partner programs that offer co-marketing (some link partners from their site).

### 3. Unlinked brand-mention reclamation
- Set a Google Alert / use a free mention tool for "ShelbyAI" / "shelby-ai.com".
- When someone mentions you without linking, ask for the link. Highest-conversion outreach there is.

### 4. Expert-quote platforms (HARO-style)
- Sign up: Featured (formerly HARO), Qwoted, Help a B2B Writer, SourceBottle.
- Answer 3–5 AI/SaaS/marketing queries per week with a real, specific quote from your testing. Journalists link the source. Great DA links.

### 5. Original data / mini-studies (digital PR)
You already have the raw material from hands-on testing. Publish 1 small study, e.g.:
- "We tracked the real price-per-output of 15 AI writing tools" or "How AI tool pricing changed in 2026."
- Pitch to AI/marketing newsletters + journalists. Data studies earn the most links.

### 6. Guest posts
- Target AI/marketing/solopreneur blogs that accept contributors. Pitch a non-promotional, genuinely useful angle (e.g., "How to build a $100/mo AI stack"), link naturally to 1 relevant guide.
- 1–2 quality guest posts/month > many low-quality ones.

### 7. Broken-link building
- Find dead links on resource pages about "AI tools / reviews" (use a free broken-link checker on roundup pages).
- Email the page owner: "You link to X which is dead — here's a current equivalent." Offer your matching review/guide.

### 8. Comparison/winner outreach
- When a tool is a "winner" in one of your comparisons, tell their team — they often share/link it.

### 9. Community presence (value-first, links sparingly & per platform rules)
- Quora, relevant Discord/Slack communities, niche Facebook groups, Indie Hackers: answer questions with genuine help; link only where allowed and relevant.
- **Reddit: participate for reputation only — do NOT post shelby-ai.com links (permanent-ban risk).**

## Outreach templates

**A. Unlinked mention → link**
> Subject: Quick fix on your [Topic] piece
> Hi [Name], thanks for mentioning ShelbyAI in [article]! Would you be open to linking the mention to our [relevant page]? Makes it easier for readers to find. Either way, appreciate the shout-out.

**B. Tool vendor (positive review)**
> Subject: Hands-on [Tool] review — feel free to use a quote
> Hi [Name], we spent 14 days testing [Tool] and published an honest review: [URL]. We rated it [X]/5 and highlighted [strength]. You're welcome to quote/link it on your site or socials — here's a ready pull-quote: "[quote]".

**C. Broken link**
> Subject: Broken link on [page]
> Hi [Name], on [URL] the link to [dead resource] returns a 404. We have a current, in-depth alternative that fits that spot: [URL]. Either way, wanted to flag the dead link.

**D. Guest post pitch**
> Subject: Guest post idea: [specific angle]
> Hi [Name], I run ShelbyAI (independent AI-tool reviews, hands-on tested). I'd love to write "[Title]" for [Blog] — practical, non-promotional, with original data from our testing. Outline: [3 bullets]. Want me to send a draft?

## Tracking (simple sheet)
Columns: Target | Type | Contact | Date sent | Follow-up date | Status | Live link | DA. Follow up once after 5–7 days; then drop.

## 90-day cadence & KPIs
- **Weekly:** 5 directory submissions, 5 HARO answers, 5 outreach emails, 1 community-value post.
- **Monthly:** 1 guest post, 1 mini data-study or original asset, review pipeline of new linkable content.
- **KPIs:** referring domains (Search Console → Links), # non-branded queries with impressions, avg position trend, branded-vs-non-branded click ratio. Re-pull GSC monthly to measure.

## What to expect
Realistically: first ranking movement on long-tail/striking-distance terms in **6–10 weeks**; meaningful non-branded traffic in **3–6 months** of consistent effort. Authority compounds — the first 10 quality links are the hardest.
