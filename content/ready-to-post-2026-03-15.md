**Title:** `My full AI content pipeline: from research to published video in 2 hours`

**Body:**
```
I've been refining my AI-assisted content pipeline for a few months now. Here's the exact workflow I use to go from idea to published blog post + video in about 2 hours.

## The 6-step pipeline

**Step 1: Keyword research (15 min)**
Use Mangools to find a keyword with decent volume and low difficulty. Check Google Search Console for related queries I'm already getting impressions on.

**Step 2: First draft (30 min)**
Give ChatGPT the keyword, target audience, and outline structure. It produces a solid 1500-word first draft. Not publishable, but a great starting point.

**Step 3: Rewrite & add depth (30 min)**
Paste into Claude and ask it to expand with specific examples, fix the generic AI tone, and add original insights. Claude is noticeably better at long-form than ChatGPT.

**Step 4: SEO optimization (15 min)**
Run through Surfer SEO or manually check keyword placement, headings, internal links. Add FAQ section targeting featured snippets.

**Step 5: Polish (10 min)**
Grammarly free tier for grammar/spelling. Quick manual read for flow. Add images/screenshots.

**Step 6: Video repurposing (20 min)**
Paste the finished post into Pictory. It auto-generates a video with captions from your text. Quick edit for pacing, export, upload to YouTube.

## Total time: ~2 hours
## Output: 1 blog post (2000-3000 words) + 1 video

**Before this pipeline:** I was spending 5-6 hours per piece and not doing video at all.

**The key insight:** AI doesn't replace the writing — it replaces the blank page. Every step still needs human editing and judgment. But going from blank page to first draft in 30 minutes instead of 2 hours changes everything.

**Tools used:** ChatGPT ($20/mo), Claude ($20/mo), Mangools ($30/mo), Pictory ($19/mo), Grammarly (free), Surfer SEO (optional, $89/mo)

I wrote detailed reviews of all these tools with pricing breakdowns: [shelby-ai.com](https://shelby-ai.com)
```
