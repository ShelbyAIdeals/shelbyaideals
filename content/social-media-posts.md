### Reddit Post 3 — r/content_marketing

**Title:** How I set up an AI-assisted content pipeline that cut my production time by 60%

**Body:**

Wanted to share the content workflow I've been refining over the past few months. Went from spending 6-8 hours per article to about 2-3 hours, and the quality actually improved because I have more time for editing and research.

**The workflow:**

**Step 1: Topic research + outline (20 min)**
I use ChatGPT to generate topic ideas based on keyword gaps I find in Google Search Console. Then I have it create a detailed outline with H2/H3 structure, key points per section, and questions to answer. I always modify the outline manually — AI suggestions are a starting point, not a final plan.

**Step 2: First draft (30 min)**
I feed the outline into Claude (or Copy.ai if I need a specific format like a listicle) and generate a first draft. Important: I never publish AI drafts as-is. The draft is raw material. It gives me something to edit rather than staring at a blank page.

**Step 3: SEO optimization (20 min)**
I run the draft through Surfer SEO to check keyword density, content structure, and gaps compared to top-ranking articles. This usually means adding 2-3 sections I hadn't thought of and adjusting headings.

**Step 4: Human editing (45-60 min)**
This is where the real work happens. I rewrite generic AI language, add personal experience and opinions, verify all facts and statistics, add real examples, and cut filler. Grammarly catches the mechanical stuff. The strategic editing is all manual.

**Step 5: Visuals + repurposing (15 min)**
Canva for featured images and social graphics. If it's a high-performing piece, I run it through Pictory to generate a video version for social.

**Step 6: Publish + distribute (10 min)**
Make.com handles the automation — publishes to CMS, auto-posts excerpts to social channels, sends to email subscribers.

**Total cost of the stack:** About $100-150/mo depending on which tools you choose. ChatGPT Plus ($20) + Surfer SEO ($89) is the most expensive part. You can do this cheaper by dropping Surfer and using free SEO tools.

**What I learned:**

- AI is best at getting you from 0 to 60%. The last 40% (editing, fact-checking, adding original insight) is what makes content worth reading.
- The biggest time savings aren't in writing — they're in research, outlining, and distribution automation.
- Don't try to automate quality. Automate the boring parts (formatting, scheduling, cross-posting) and spend your time on the parts that actually need a human brain.
