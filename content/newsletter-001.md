## Body

Hey there,

If I had to start a business tomorrow with zero content, zero traffic, and zero audience — these are the 5 AI tools I'd set up before anything else. Not the trendiest picks. The ones that actually move the needle early on.

Here's my stack, in the order I'd buy them.

---

### 1. Mangools — Get Found on Google ($29/mo)

Most new businesses completely ignore SEO for the first year, then wonder why nobody finds them. Mangools is how you skip that mistake.

It does keyword research, rank tracking, and backlink analysis — basically what SEMrush does, but at a fraction of the price. SEMrush starts at $140/mo and is built for agencies managing dozens of clients. If you're a solo founder or small team, that's overkill. Mangools gives you 90% of what matters for $29/mo and the interface is dead simple. I'd rather spend the $110/mo savings on ads or content.

[Read the full SEO tools breakdown on ShelbyAI](https://shelby-ai.com/reviews/semrush-review/)

**[Try Mangools ($29/mo)](https://mangools.com/a/a69b085f86aee0888864a82a9)**

---

### 2. Pictory — Turn Blog Posts Into Video ($19/mo)

You write a blog post. Pictory turns it into a short video with stock footage, captions, and AI voiceover. Done in minutes, not hours.

Is it going to win a film award? No. But it gives you a video presence on YouTube, LinkedIn, and social media without hiring an editor or learning Premiere Pro. For a new business trying to be everywhere at once, that's a genuine multiplier. The Starter plan at $19/mo gets you 30 videos a month, which is more than enough.

[Read our full Pictory review](https://shelby-ai.com/reviews/pictory-review/)

**[Try Pictory ($19/mo)](https://pictory.ai?ref=fran26)**

---

### 3. Jasper AI — Write Marketing Content Fast ($49/mo)

Jasper isn't a "write my blog post for me" button — the best use is drafting marketing copy at speed. Ad variations, email sequences, landing pages, social posts. The brand voice feature is the real differentiator: you train it on your tone once, and every output sounds like you wrote it.

The honest trade-off: at $49/mo, Jasper is expensive for a solopreneur writing one post a week. If that's you, ChatGPT is fine. But if you're publishing daily across multiple channels and need consistent brand voice, Jasper pays for itself in time saved.

[Read our full Jasper AI review](https://shelby-ai.com/reviews/jasper-ai-review/)

**[Try Jasper AI ($49/mo)](https://www.jasper.ai/)**

---

### 4. Make.com — Automate the Boring Stuff (Free to start)

Make connects your apps and automates repetitive workflows with a visual drag-and-drop builder. New lead comes in? Auto-add to CRM, send a welcome email, notify Slack. Blog post published? Auto-share to social, update your sitemap, ping Google.

The reason I'd pick Make over Zapier: Make's free plan includes 1,000 operations/month (enough for a new business), and the paid plans start at $9/mo. Zapier charges 3-5x more for equivalent workflows. The visual builder is also genuinely better for complex multi-step automations — you can see exactly what happens at each stage.

[Read our full Make.com review](https://shelby-ai.com/reviews/make-review/)

**[Try Make.com (free plan available)](https://www.make.com/)**

---

### 5. ElevenLabs — Professional Voice Without a Mic ($0-22/mo)

ElevenLabs generates realistic AI voiceovers, narration, and audio content. Think podcast intros, video narration, audiobook chapters, or course material — all without recording a single word yourself.

What sets it apart from other text-to-speech tools is that it doesn't sound robotic. The voices have natural pacing, emotion, and inflection. They offer a free tier to test it out, and the Starter plan at $5/mo gives you enough minutes for most small business needs. If you're creating any kind of audio or video content, this saves hours of recording and editing.

[Browse our AI video and audio tool roundups](https://shelby-ai.com/best/best-ai-video-tools-creators/)

**[Try ElevenLabs (free tier available)](https://try.elevenlabs.io/3vu715wo9f9y)**

### Total cost for the full stack: ~$97/mo

That gets you SEO, video, content writing, automation, and professional audio. A year ago, you'd need a team of 3-4 people to cover all of that. Now it's one person and five subscriptions.
