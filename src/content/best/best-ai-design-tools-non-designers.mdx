### How We Evaluated

Our evaluation focused on several core criteria to ensure these tools meet the needs of non-designers. ## #1: Canva AI — Best all-in-one design tool for non-designers

**Why it earned this rank:** Canva AI stands out for its comprehensive suite of design tools that cater to everything from social media graphics to presentations. Its intuitive drag-and-drop interface and extensive library of templates make it a favorite among beginners and freelancers.

**Key features:**
- **Drag-and-drop editor:** Simplifies the design process with an intuitive interface.
- **Extensive template library:** Thousands of templates for diverse needs.
- **AI-powered design suggestions:** Provides smart recommendations to enhance designs.
- **Collaboration tools:** Allows teams to work together in real-time. (Marketing teams should also see the [best AI tools for marketing teams](/best/best-ai-tools-marketing-teams).)

**Pricing:** Canva AI offers a free tier with essential tools, while its Pro plan at $12.99/month or $119.99/year offers advanced features like brand kits and priority support.

**Who it is for:** Ideal for beginners and freelancers who need a versatile design tool without a steep learning curve.

## #2: Visme — Ideal for creating engaging presentations

**Why it earned this rank:** Visme excels in creating visually appealing presentations with interactive elements. Its focus on storytelling through design makes it perfect for users looking to create impactful presentations.

**Key features:**
- **Interactive presentation features:** Embed videos, polls, and quizzes.
- **Branding tools:** Customize presentations to fit brand aesthetics.
- **Data visualization:** Create infographics and detailed charts easily.
- **Collaboration capabilities:** Share and edit presentations with team members.

**Pricing:** Visme starts at $29/month, with an annual plan providing additional savings. The business plan offers comprehensive analytics and premium support.

**Who it is for:** Best suited for professionals who frequently create presentations and need advanced customization options.

## #3: Adobe Express — Great for quick social media graphics

**Why it earned this rank:** Adobe Express provides powerful tools specifically for creating compelling social media content. Its integration with Adobe's ecosystem allows users to leverage a wide range of resources.

**Key features:**
- **Social media templates:** Pre-designed templates optimized for various platforms.
- **Adobe Stock integration:** Access to a vast library of stock photos and illustrations.
- **Quick actions:** Resize, crop, and enhance photos swiftly.
- **Typography tools:** Advanced font options for stylish text overlays.

**Pricing:** Adobe Express is free to use, with the Premium plan at $9.99/month unlocking additional features and resources.

**Who it is for:** Social media managers looking for a quick and efficient way to produce high-quality graphics.

## #4: Crello — Best for ad creatives on a budget

**Why it earned this rank:** Crello offers a cost-effective solution for creating marketing materials. Its user-friendly platform and competitive pricing make it accessible for those on a budget.

**Key features:**
- **Ad templates:** Designed for a variety of advertising formats.
- **Animation tools:** Add motion to designs with ease.
- **Free stock images:** Access to a large library of free visuals.
- **Collaborative editing:** Work with others to refine designs.

**Pricing:** Crello offers a free version, with its Pro plan available at $10/month for enhanced features and more storage.

**Who it is for:** Ideal for budget-conscious users who need to create professional ad content without extensive costs.

## #5: Desygner — Flexible design tool for various formats

**Why it earned this rank:** Desygner provides flexibility across multiple design types, including social media, documents, and marketing materials. Its ease of use is complemented by robust customization features.

**Key features:**
- **Multi-format support:** Design across social media, print, and digital platforms.
- **Custom branding kits:** Maintain consistency with brand templates.
- **Royalty-free resources:** Utilize free photos and graphics.
- **PDF editor:** Edit and enhance PDF documents directly.

**Pricing:** Desygner's free version is quite capable, with a Pro+ plan at $9.95/month for additional tools and resources.

**Who it is for:** Suitable for users with diverse design needs across different media types.

## #6: GIMP — Free and open-source design software

**Why it earned this rank:** GIMP is included for its powerful open-source editing capabilities. Despite a steeper learning curve, it offers comprehensive features for those willing to invest the time.

**Key features:**
- **Advanced image manipulation:** Detailed editing tools for professional results.
- **Customizable interface:** Tailor the workspace to specific workflow needs.
- **Plugin support:** Extend functionality with community-created plugins.
- **Broad file format support:** Compatible with most image formats.

**Pricing:** Completely free, making it an ideal option for those seeking a budget-friendly tool.

**Who it is for:** Best for tech-savvy users who appreciate open-source software and have the patience to learn its intricacies.

### What We Left Out

Several tools, while promising, didn't make the list due to specific shortcomings. **Stencil** offers excellent simplicity but lacks the depth needed for more complex projects. **Snappa** is user-friendly but its pricing doesn't offer the best value compared to more feature-rich options. **Piktochart** excels in infographics but isn't versatile enough for broader design needs. **RelayThat** provides strong brand automation but falls short in customizability, which is crucial for many users. Lastly, **Design Wizard** is limited by its relatively small template library, restricting creativity.
