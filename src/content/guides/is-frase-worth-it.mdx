Frase combines AI writing with SEO content optimization in one tool. You research a keyword, get a detailed content brief based on top-ranking pages, write or generate your article with AI assistance, and optimize it against SEO benchmarks — all in the same interface.

For content writers, this is appealing because it eliminates the multi-tool juggle. You do not need one tool for research, another for writing, and a third for SEO scoring. Frase aims to handle all three. The question is whether it does each job well enough to justify the subscription, or whether you are better off with dedicated tools for each task.

This guide answers that question specifically for content writers — freelancers, bloggers, in-house content teams — who write SEO-focused content regularly. For the full feature walkthrough, see our [Frase review](/reviews/frase-review).

## What Frase Actually Does

Frase has three core modules:

1. **Content Research.** Enter a target keyword, and Frase pulls data from the top 20 Google results. It extracts the questions people ask (from People Also Ask, Quora, Reddit), the common headings used by ranking pages, key statistics, and relevant topics. You get a comprehensive research brief without opening a dozen browser tabs.

2. **AI Writing.** Frase includes a built-in AI writer that generates outlines, paragraphs, intros, conclusions, and full article sections. You can write alongside the AI — type your own content and let the AI expand, rephrase, or fill gaps. The AI writer is trained to follow the SEO brief, so its output is informed by what the ranking pages cover.

3. **Content Optimization.** As you write, Frase scores your content against the top-ranking pages for your keyword. It shows a topic score that reflects how comprehensively your article covers the key themes, terms, and entities that Google expects to see. Similar to Surfer SEO's content score, but with a different algorithmic approach.

4. **Content Analytics (Pro plan).** Tracks your published content's performance and identifies pages that need updating — declining traffic, missing topics, content decay. This is a newer feature that helps with content maintenance, not just creation.

## Who Frase Is Best For

### Freelance SEO Content Writers

If you write SEO content for clients, Frase is arguably the best value tool available. For $15/mo, you get content research, AI writing assistance, and SEO optimization. Delivering articles with a Frase optimization score gives clients confidence in the SEO quality. The research module alone saves 30-60 minutes per article by consolidating competitor analysis into one view.

**The freelancer math:** If Frase helps you write one additional article per week by speeding up your research and drafting process, and you charge $200-500 per article, the $15/mo subscription pays for itself many times over.

### Solo Bloggers and Niche Site Owners

You wear every hat — researcher, writer, SEO strategist, editor, publisher. Frase reduces that to one interface for the research-write-optimize workflow. For a solo operator producing 2-4 articles per month, the Solo plan covers your needs without overpaying for team features you do not use.

### Content Writers Transitioning to SEO Writing

If you are a good writer who wants to add SEO skills, Frase is an excellent learning tool. The research briefs teach you what Google values for any keyword. The optimization score gives you real-time feedback on coverage. Over time, you internalize SEO best practices by seeing what the algorithm rewards.

### Small Content Teams on a Budget

The Basic plan at $45/mo gives 30 documents — enough for a small team of 2-3 writers producing content regularly. Compare that to Surfer SEO at $89/mo for 30 articles on their Essential plan. Frase delivers similar (though not identical) optimization capability at half the price, and includes AI writing that Surfer charges extra for.

## Who Should Skip Frase

### Writers Who Need Only AI Generation (Not SEO)

If you do not write SEO content — you write newsletters, social media, ad copy, fiction, or creative content — Frase's value proposition does not apply. Its AI writer is decent but not best-in-class for pure writing tasks. [Copy.ai](/reviews/copy-ai-review) or [Jasper](/reviews/jasper-ai-review) are better pure AI writing tools with more templates and content types.

### Teams That Need the Deepest SEO Analysis

Frase's optimization is good — we would say 80% as detailed as Surfer SEO. But that last 20% matters for highly competitive keywords where every on-page signal counts. If you are competing for keywords with 50,000+ monthly searches against established domains, Surfer's deeper NLP analysis and more granular scoring give you an edge that Frase does not quite match. Read our [Surfer SEO buying guide](/guides/is-surfer-seo-worth-it) for a detailed comparison.

### High-Volume Content Operations

If you produce 50+ articles per month with a large writing team, Frase's Team plan at $115/mo works but may not scale as smoothly as enterprise-grade tools. At that volume, the investment in Surfer SEO plus a dedicated AI writing tool often provides a more robust workflow with better collaboration features.

### People Who Need a One-Day Free Trial to Test

Frase's free trial is frustratingly short — one day with one document. That is barely enough time to evaluate the tool properly. The Solo plan at $15/mo is low-risk enough that most writers subscribe for a month to test it properly and cancel if it does not fit. But if you need an extended trial period, Surfer SEO offers a longer evaluation window.

## Tips for Content Writers Using Frase

1. **Use the research brief before writing, every time.** Even if you think you know a topic well, Frase's competitor analysis surfaces angles and questions you would miss. The "People Also Ask" extraction alone is worth the subscription.

2. **Do not rely entirely on AI-generated content.** Use Frase's AI writer to draft sections, generate outlines, and overcome blank-page paralysis. Then rewrite in your voice. AI-generated content that is not edited reads like AI-generated content — and readers (and Google) can tell.

3. **Aim for a topic score of 70+.** Frase's optimization score measures how comprehensively you cover the key themes. Scores below 60 often mean you are missing important subtopics. Scores above 80 mean you have covered everything the competition covers and then some.

4. **Create documents in batches.** On the Solo plan with 10 documents, create all your research briefs for the month early. You can continue writing and editing existing documents throughout the month — the credit is consumed on creation.

5. **Use the "Compare" feature.** Frase lets you see exactly what each ranking competitor covers. Identify the gaps — topics that only 1-2 competitors mention well — and cover them thoroughly. That is where you differentiate your content from the crowd.
