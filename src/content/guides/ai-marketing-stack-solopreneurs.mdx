You are your entire marketing department. SEO, content, social media, email, video, paid ads, analytics, and brand design — all of it falls on you. That is the reality of marketing as a solopreneur, and it is also the reason most solo businesses underinvest in marketing. There simply are not enough hours.

AI tools solve the time problem. Not by doing everything perfectly, but by compressing the production work so you spend more time on strategy and less time on execution. The right marketing stack turns a solo operator into a one-person marketing team that produces at the volume of a small agency.

This guide is different from our [general solopreneur AI stack guide](/guides/solopreneur-ai-stack), which covers productivity tools across your entire business. This guide focuses specifically on marketing — the tools that help you get found, build an audience, and convert visitors into customers.

## The Five Pillars of Solo Marketing

Before picking tools, understand the five jobs your marketing stack needs to cover.

### 1. SEO and Organic Discovery

People need to find you. For most solopreneurs, organic search is the most sustainable traffic source — it compounds over time, does not require daily effort once content ranks, and costs nothing per click. Your stack needs keyword research and content optimization.

### 2. Content Creation

Blog posts, landing pages, email newsletters, social media posts, ad copy, product descriptions. Marketing runs on words. Your stack needs AI writing that handles multiple content formats while maintaining your brand voice.

### 3. Video and Audio

Video is the highest-engagement format on every platform. Audio (podcasts, voice content) is growing. Your stack needs to produce both without requiring filming or recording skills.

### 4. Design and Visual Content

Social graphics, thumbnails, ad creatives, branded templates. Every piece of content needs a visual component. Your stack needs design capability that does not require a designer.

### 5. Automation and Distribution

Publishing, scheduling, email sequences, cross-posting, reporting. The repetitive mechanics of distributing your content. Your stack needs to automate the busywork.

## The 7 Tools That Cover Everything

### 1. Frase — Content Research + Writing + SEO ($15/mo)

[Frase](https://www.frase.io/?via=shelby-ai) is the single highest-value tool for solo marketers. It combines three functions that would otherwise require three separate subscriptions: content research, AI writing, and SEO optimization.

**How solopreneurs use it for marketing:**
- **Keyword research briefs.** Enter a target keyword, get an instant analysis of what the top-ranking pages cover — headings, topics, questions, word counts. This is your content blueprint.
- **AI-assisted writing.** Generate article outlines, section drafts, intros, and conclusions. You edit and add your expertise. The AI handles the blank-page problem.
- **SEO optimization scoring.** As you write, a real-time score shows how your content compares to top-ranking pages. Hit 70+ and you have covered the essential topics.

**Why it is the cornerstone:** For $15/mo, Frase replaces a separate research tool, a separate AI writer, and a separate SEO optimizer. No other tool offers this combination at this price. See our [Frase buying guide](/guides/is-frase-worth-it) for the detailed analysis.

**When to upgrade:** If you need deeper keyword clustering and more granular optimization data, add [Mangools](/reviews/mangools-review) for keyword research and consider upgrading to [Surfer SEO](/reviews/surfer-seo-review) for optimization when your content volume justifies it.

### 2. Mangools — Keyword Research and Rank Tracking ($29/mo)

[Mangools](https://mangools.com#a69b085f86aee0888864a82a9) handles the strategic side of SEO — finding the right keywords to target, analyzing competition, and tracking where you rank over time.

**How solopreneurs use it:**
- **Keyword research.** Find keywords your audience searches for, filtered by difficulty and volume. Target low-competition, high-intent keywords where a solo operator can realistically rank.
- **SERP analysis.** See exactly who ranks for your target keywords and how strong their pages are. Identify gaps where your content can compete.
- **Rank tracking.** Monitor your positions over time. Know which content is climbing, which is stable, and which needs updating.
- **Content calendar planning.** Use keyword data to plan your content calendar weeks in advance. Prioritize topics by opportunity.

**Why solopreneurs need it alongside Frase:** Frase optimizes individual articles. Mangools informs your content strategy — which articles to write in the first place. Together, they cover the full SEO workflow: strategy (Mangools) plus execution (Frase).

### 3. Copy.ai — Marketing Copy for Everything ($36/mo)

[Copy.ai](/reviews/copy-ai-review) generates the non-SEO marketing content that keeps your business visible — ad copy, social posts, email campaigns, product descriptions, and sales pages.

**How solopreneurs use it:**
- **Social media batching.** Generate a week of social posts in 20 minutes. Give Copy.ai your topic and audience, get 15-20 post variations across platforms.
- **Email marketing.** Draft welcome sequences, promotional emails, and newsletter content. Copy.ai handles the structure and copy; you add personalization and links.
- **Ad copy variations.** Generate 10 Facebook ad headlines in 30 seconds for A/B testing. More variations tested means lower ad costs.
- **Landing page copy.** Create compelling headlines, benefit sections, and calls to action for your sales and landing pages.

**Why it complements Frase:** Frase is for long-form SEO content. Copy.ai is for short-form marketing content. Different tools for different content types. There is minimal overlap.

### 4. Pictory — Blog Posts to Social Video ($19/mo)

[Pictory](https://pictory.ai?ref=fran26) turns your written content into video. For solopreneurs, this means every blog post you write becomes a social media video, YouTube clip, and visual content piece — without filming anything.

**How solopreneurs use it:**
- **Blog-to-social-video.** Publish a blog post, paste it into Pictory, get a 60-second video for Instagram Reels, TikTok, and YouTube Shorts. Triple your content distribution from one piece of writing.
- **Key insight clips.** Pull the most important points from long-form content and create standalone tip videos.
- **Promotional videos.** Create product or service promotion videos from your marketing copy.

**Why video matters for solo marketing:** Video gets 2-3x more engagement than text or image posts on every social platform. If you are not producing video, you are leaving engagement on the table. Pictory eliminates the production barrier. Read our [Pictory buying guide](/guides/is-pictory-worth-it).

### 5. ElevenLabs — Professional Voice for All Content ($5/mo)

[ElevenLabs](https://try.elevenlabs.io/3vu715wo9f9y) adds professional voiceover to your video content, and at $5/mo for 30 minutes of audio, it is the cheapest way to sound professional.

**How solopreneurs use it:**

**The ElevenLabs + Pictory combo:** Generate voiceover in ElevenLabs, import into Pictory. Total cost: $24/mo for professional, narrated video content. That combination replaces what used to require a videographer, a voice actor, and an editor.

### 6. Canva Pro — Design Everything ($13/mo)

Canva Pro handles the visual side of your marketing — social graphics, ad creatives, video thumbnails, email headers, presentation decks, and brand templates.

**How solopreneurs use it:**
- **Social graphics.** Create platform-optimized images for every post. Use Magic Resize to adapt one design to Instagram, LinkedIn, Twitter, and Pinterest simultaneously.
- **Video thumbnails.** Design click-worthy YouTube and blog thumbnails.
- **Ad creatives.** Build display ad graphics and social ad images from templates.
- **Brand consistency.** Upload your logo, set brand colors and fonts, and every future design stays on-brand automatically.
- **Simple video.** Animated social clips, product showcases, and slideshow-style videos directly in Canva.

**Why Canva earns its place:** Every piece of marketing content needs a visual. Canva ensures your visuals are professional and consistent without requiring design skills. At $13/mo, it is the best value design tool available.

### 7. Make.com — Automate the Busywork ($9/mo)

[Make.com](/reviews/make-review) connects your tools and automates the repetitive distribution, scheduling, and reporting tasks that eat solopreneur time.

**How solopreneurs use it:**
- **Social scheduling.** Publish a blog post, and Make.com automatically schedules social media promotions across platforms on your predetermined schedule.
- **Email triggers.** Someone fills out your contact form? Automatic welcome email, CRM entry, and notification.
- **Content repurposing pipeline.** New blog post triggers: create social graphics in Canva, schedule posts, add to newsletter queue, update content tracker.
- **Reporting.** Pull analytics from Google Analytics, social platforms, and email tools into a single dashboard or spreadsheet.

**The automation principle:** Any task you do the same way more than three times should be automated. Make.com handles the connective tissue between your other tools.

## Weekly Marketing Workflow (2 Hours Total)

Here is how a solopreneur uses the Advanced Stack in a real weekly marketing routine.

**Monday: Content Planning (20 min)**
- Check Mangools for keyword opportunities and ranking changes.
- Pick one keyword to target this week.
- Create a Frase document and research brief for that keyword.

**Tuesday: Content Creation (45 min)**
- Write the article in Frase with AI assistance and optimization scoring.
- Aim for 1,500-2,500 words with a topic score of 70+.
- Publish to your blog.

**Wednesday: Video and Design (30 min)**
- Paste the blog post into Pictory. Generate a 60-second social video and a 3-minute YouTube version.
- Generate voiceover in ElevenLabs and add to videos.
- Create the blog thumbnail and social graphics in Canva.

**Thursday: Distribution (15 min)**
- Use Copy.ai to generate 5-7 social posts from the blog content — variations for each platform.
- Queue them in your social scheduler (or let Make.com handle distribution automatically).
- Draft a newsletter blurb with Copy.ai. Add to your email tool.

**Friday: Automation Check (10 min)**
- Review Make.com automation logs. Confirm posts went out, emails were sent, tracking is working.
- Check analytics from the previous week's content.
- Note what to adjust next week.

**Total weekly time: approximately 2 hours.** That 2-hour routine covers blog content, video content, social media, email marketing, and SEO — the five pillars of marketing. Without AI tools, this same output would take 10-15 hours per week.

## What We Left Out (and Why)

**Semrush ($130/mo).** The industry standard for SEO. But at $130/mo, it costs more than our entire Advanced Stack. Mangools + Frase covers 80% of Semrush's utility for solo marketers at a fraction of the cost. Upgrade when your organic traffic is mature enough to justify enterprise-grade analysis.

**Jasper ($49/mo).** Excellent AI writer, but Copy.ai does the same job for $36/mo with comparable quality for short-form marketing content. Jasper is better for agencies managing multiple brand voices. For a solo operator with one brand, Copy.ai is the better value.

**Midjourney ($10/mo).** Stunning AI art, but Canva Pro handles practical marketing design (social graphics, thumbnails, ad creatives) and includes AI image generation. Adding Midjourney on top creates overlap.

**Buffer / Hootsuite (social schedulers).** Make.com can handle social scheduling as part of broader automation workflows. If you need a dedicated scheduler, these are solid choices, but they add another subscription to manage.
