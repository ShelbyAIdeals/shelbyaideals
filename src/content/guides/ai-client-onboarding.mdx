# AI Client Onboarding Checklist: Shareable Template for Freelancers & Agencies

This guide gives you a **copyable onboarding checklist**, a visual workflow, and step-by-step instructions for building an automated client onboarding system using AI tools. Whether you're a freelancer, agency, or consultant, you can adapt this template to your workflow.

---

## The Checklist (Copy This)

Use this checklist as your onboarding template. Copy it into your project management tool, share it with your team, or send it directly to clients.

### Phase 1: Discovery & Alignment
- [ ] Send welcome email with intro packet
- [ ] Schedule discovery call via Calendly
- [ ] Complete discovery questionnaire (Google Forms)
- [ ] Define project scope, deliverables, and timeline
- [ ] Identify client's existing tools and tech stack
- [ ] Agree on communication channels (Slack, email, etc.)

### Phase 2: Contracts & Access
- [ ] Send contract/SOW for e-signature
- [ ] Receive signed contract
- [ ] Send invoice for first payment/retainer
- [ ] Confirm payment received
- [ ] Set up shared folder (Google Drive or Dropbox)
- [ ] Request client access credentials (logins, API keys, brand assets)

### Phase 3: Setup & Configuration
- [ ] Create client project in project management tool (Asana, Notion, ClickUp)
- [ ] Set up automation workflows (Zapier or Make.com)
- [ ] Configure reporting dashboard or shared status doc
- [ ] Add client to relevant communication channels
- [ ] Schedule recurring check-in meetings

### Phase 4: Kickoff & Handoff
- [ ] Conduct kickoff meeting (record with Otter.ai or similar)
- [ ] Share project timeline and milestones
- [ ] Confirm roles and responsibilities (who approves what)
- [ ] Deliver first draft or initial deliverable
- [ ] Collect feedback and adjust workflow

## Step 1: Automate the Welcome Sequence

When a new client signs up or confirms engagement, trigger an automated welcome sequence:

1. **Welcome email** — Use [Make.com](/reviews/make-review/) or [Zapier](/comparisons/zapier-vs-make/) to send a personalized welcome email with your intro packet, questionnaire link, and Calendly booking link
2. **Discovery questionnaire** — Google Forms captures project goals, existing tools, brand guidelines, and preferences
3. **Automatic task creation** — When the form is submitted, create a project in Asana with pre-populated tasks from your onboarding template

This sequence should run without manual intervention. Set it up once, and every new client gets the same professional experience. For choosing between Zapier and Make.com for this automation, see our detailed [Zapier vs Make comparison](/comparisons/zapier-vs-make/).

## Step 2: Streamline Scheduling and Meetings

Use **Calendly** to eliminate scheduling back-and-forth:

- Set up specific meeting types: Discovery Call (30 min), Kickoff Meeting (60 min), Weekly Check-in (15 min)
- Sync with your calendar to show real availability
- Add buffer times between meetings
- Include Zoom/Google Meet links automatically
- Send automatic reminders 24 hours and 1 hour before the meeting

For meeting transcription and notes, tools like [Otter.ai](/reviews/otter-ai-review/) can automatically capture action items from your discovery and kickoff calls.

## Step 3: Handle Contracts and Payments

Automate the paperwork:

- Use a contract template with e-signature (DocuSign, PandaDoc, or HelloSign)
- Trigger contract delivery automatically after the discovery call
- Set up payment automation with Stripe or PayPal
- Use Zapier/Make.com to notify you when both contract and payment are complete, which triggers the setup phase

## Step 4: Set Up the Client Workspace

Once contract and payment are confirmed:

1. **Create the project** — Use Asana templates to generate a pre-populated project with standard onboarding tasks, due dates, and assignees
2. **Share the folder** — Create a Google Drive folder structure: /Client Name/Briefs, /Assets, /Deliverables, /Reports
3. **Configure automations** — Set up status update notifications, deadline reminders, and deliverable review triggers
4. **Add communication channels** — Create a shared Slack channel or set up email threads

For project management tools that integrate well with automation, see our [Notion AI vs ClickUp vs Monday comparison](/comparisons/notion-ai-vs-clickup-vs-monday/).

## Step 5: Conduct the Kickoff and Begin Work

The kickoff meeting is where alignment happens:

- Walk through the project timeline and milestones
- Confirm who approves deliverables and the approval process
- Set expectations for response times and communication cadence
- Share the onboarding checklist (from above) so the client knows what's been completed and what's next

After the kickoff, deliver your first draft or initial deliverable within 7-14 days to build momentum and trust.

## Adapting This for Different Roles

### For Freelancers
Keep it simple. Use the free tiers of Calendly, Google Workspace, and Make.com or Zapier. Focus on automating the welcome email, questionnaire, and task creation. Skip the project management tool if you manage fewer than 3 clients at once — a shared Google Doc works fine.

### For Agencies
Scale the template across team members. Use Asana or ClickUp with team assignments, add approval workflows for deliverables, and create role-specific onboarding tracks (designer onboarding vs copywriter onboarding). For a broader toolkit, see our [solopreneur AI stack guide](/guides/solopreneur-ai-stack/).

### For Consultants
Emphasize the discovery and alignment phases. Your clients may need more hand-holding on scope definition and expectations. Add a "client education" step where you share relevant resources, case studies, or onboarding videos before the kickoff call.

## Client Alignment and Approvals

The most common onboarding failure is **misaligned expectations**. Build these checks into your process:

- **Scope confirmation document** — Before starting work, have the client sign off on a one-page scope summary: deliverables, timeline, revision rounds, and what's out of scope
- **Approval workflow** — Define who reviews and approves each deliverable, and how long they have to respond (e.g., 48-hour turnaround on feedback)
- **Escalation path** — If a client is unresponsive, have a predefined follow-up sequence: Day 3 reminder, Day 7 escalation, Day 14 pause notice

## FAQ

### How long does it take to set up this system?

2-4 hours for the basic automation (welcome email + questionnaire + task creation). A full system with contracts, payments, and reporting takes a day or two.

### Can I run this entirely on free tools?

Yes. Make.com's free tier (1,000 operations/month), Calendly's free plan, Asana's free tier, and Google Workspace's free tools handle basic onboarding for 1-3 clients per month. For higher volume, expect to spend $15-30/month total.

### What if my client isn't tech-savvy?

Keep the client-facing steps simple: a Calendly link to book, a Google Form to fill out, and a shared Google Drive folder. The automation and project management happens on your side — the client just sees a smooth, professional experience.

### How do I handle multiple clients onboarding at the same time?

Use project templates in Asana or Notion. Each new client gets a cloned template with pre-populated tasks. Automations handle the welcome sequence and task creation, so onboarding multiple clients simultaneously requires minimal additional effort.
