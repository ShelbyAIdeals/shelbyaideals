## Quick Answer: Which Tool Should You Pick?

**For podcasters and audio-heavy content:** Choose **Descript**. Its text-based editing approach lets you edit audio by editing a transcript — delete a word from the text, and it's removed from the audio. No other tool does this as well.

**For remote interviews and webinars:** Choose **Riverside**. It records each participant's audio and video locally in up to 4K, so a guest's bad WiFi won't ruin your footage.

**For social media clips and beginners:** Choose **CapCut**. It's free, loaded with templates and effects, and purpose-built for vertical video formats like TikTok, Reels, and Shorts.

## Descript: Strengths and Weaknesses

Descript's core innovation is **text-based editing** — your audio/video is transcribed, and you edit the transcript to edit the media. Delete a sentence from the text, and it's cut from the video. This approach is genuinely faster than timeline editing for podcast and talking-head content.

From our [full Descript review](/reviews/descript-review/), key strengths include:

- **AI transcription + text editing:** The fastest way to edit podcasts and interview content
- **Filler word removal:** Automatically cuts "um," "uh," and dead air
- **Studio Sound:** AI audio enhancement that cleans up non-studio recordings
- **Underlord AI co-editor:** Generates show notes, summaries, and social clips
- **Team collaboration:** Brand kits, shared templates, and multi-editor workflows

**Weaknesses:**
- **No live recording/streaming:** Descript edits existing media — it doesn't record remote interviews
- **Creator plan required for 4K:** Free and Hobbyist max out at 1080p
- **Transcription limits on lower tiers:** Free gives 60 min/mo, Hobbyist gives 10 hrs/mo

See the full [Descript pricing breakdown](/pricing/descript/) for tier details.

## Riverside: Strengths and Weaknesses

Riverside is built for **high-quality remote recording** first, editing second. Its killer feature is local recording — each participant's audio and video is captured on their own device, then synced and uploaded. Bad internet doesn't affect recording quality.

**Key strengths:**
- **4K local recording:** Studio quality even with remote guests on unreliable connections
- **Separate tracks per participant:** Full control in post-production
- **Magic Clips:** AI auto-generates short clips from long recordings
- **Live streaming:** Multistream to YouTube, LinkedIn, Instagram, X, and Twitch (Live plan)
- **Webinar hosting:** Registration, Q&A, polls, and analytics (Webinar plan)

**Weaknesses:**
- **No text-based editing:** Editing is timeline-based, not transcript-driven like Descript
- **Free tier is limited:** 2 hours of multi-track recording, 720p, with watermark
- **Higher entry price:** Pro starts at $24/mo (annual), and the useful Live plan is $34/mo

---

## CapCut: Strengths and Weaknesses

CapCut is the strongest **free video editor** available for social media content. Owned by ByteDance (TikTok's parent company), it's optimized for short-form vertical video with templates, effects, and auto-captions built in.

**Key strengths:**
- **Genuinely free:** Full-featured editor with no watermarks on most exports
- **Social-first templates:** Thousands of templates for TikTok, Reels, Shorts, and Stories
- **Auto-captions:** Fast, accurate captions with customizable styles
- **AI effects:** Background removal, style transfer, and text-to-speech
- **Mobile + desktop + web:** Edit anywhere

**Weaknesses:**
- **No recording features:** CapCut edits video — it doesn't record interviews or podcasts
- **Limited collaboration:** No shared workspaces or team features
- **Integration gaps:** Fewer workflow integrations compared to Descript
- **Pro plan details unclear:** Paid tier exists but pricing isn't prominently published

## Real-World Scenarios

### Best for Podcasters
**Winner: Descript**

Record in your preferred tool, import to Descript, and edit by editing the transcript. Remove filler words automatically, enhance audio with Studio Sound, and generate show notes with Underlord. For a complete podcast workflow, see our [AI tools for podcasters guide](/guides/ai-tools-for-podcasters/).

### Best for Remote Interviews
**Winner: Riverside**

Schedule your interview, share a link, and record. Each participant's video is captured locally in up to 4K with 48kHz audio. Even if a guest's WiFi drops, their local recording continues. Post-recording, use Magic Clips to pull highlights automatically.

### Best for Social Clips
**Winner: CapCut**

Import your footage, pick a vertical template, add auto-captions, apply effects, and export. The whole process takes under 10 minutes for a polished social clip. No account required, no payment needed. For strategies on maximizing your clips, see our [content repurposing with AI guide](/guides/repurpose-content-with-ai/).

### Best for Beginners
**Winner: CapCut**

Zero financial risk and the simplest interface of the three. Drag clips onto the timeline, add effects and text, and export. CapCut's template library does most of the creative heavy lifting, making it possible to produce good-looking content with minimal editing experience.

## Who Should Choose Each Tool

### Choose Descript if you:
- Edit podcasts or talking-head videos regularly
- Want to edit audio/video by editing text
- Need AI tools like filler removal, Studio Sound, and auto-summaries
- Work in a team that needs shared templates and brand consistency
- Want one tool for recording, editing, and publishing

### Choose Riverside if you:
- Record remote interviews, podcasts, or webinars
- Need studio-quality audio/video from remote guests
- Want to livestream to multiple platforms
- Host webinars with registration and analytics
- Need separate tracks for each participant

### Choose CapCut if you:
- Create short-form social media content (TikTok, Reels, Shorts)
- Want a free, full-featured editor with no watermarks
- Are new to video editing and want the fastest learning curve
- Need mobile editing on the go
- Don't need recording, collaboration, or workflow integrations

## Final Recommendation

These three tools serve **different primary use cases** with minimal overlap:

- **Descript** = Edit podcasts and videos by editing text. Best AI editing tools.
- **Riverside** = Record high-quality remote video. Best recording quality.
- **CapCut** = Create social clips for free. Best for beginners and budget.

Many creators use two of these together: Riverside for recording + Descript for editing, or Descript for long-form + CapCut for social clips. They're complementary, not always competitive.
