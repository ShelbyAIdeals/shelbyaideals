## How We Evaluated

Our **Zapier vs Make.com** analysis draws on documented features, official pricing pages, public user reviews, and our hands-on experience with both platforms. We focused on the criteria that matter most to creators, freelancers, and small teams.

**Evaluation criteria:**

- **Pricing value**: Cost per operation and what you get at each tier

- **Workflow complexity**: How each platform handles branching, loops, and data transformation

## Zapier: Strengths and Weaknesses

Zapier is the most well-known automation platform, with over 5,000 app integrations and a reputation for being the "duct tape of the internet." It launched in 2012 and has become the default choice for teams that need simple trigger-action automations across a wide range of tools.

**Key Strengths:**

- **Unmatched integrations**: 5,000+ app connections — if a SaaS tool exists, Zapier probably supports it
- **Simple linear editor**: The step-by-step builder is approachable for non-technical users building basic automations
- **Team features**: Shared folders, permissions, and collaboration tools for multi-user environments

**Key Weaknesses:**

- **Pricing escalates fast**: The free plan is limited to 100 tasks/month. Useful plans start at $19.99/month and scale steeply with volume
- **Linear workflow model**: Multi-step automations with branching logic require separate Zaps or workarounds
- **No visual canvas**: You cannot see the full flow at a glance — each step is a sequential list item

## Make.com: Strengths and Weaknesses

Make.com (formerly Integromat) takes a fundamentally different approach. Instead of a linear step builder, it uses a **visual canvas** where you drag, drop, and connect modules. This makes it significantly better for complex workflows with branching, loops, and data transformation.

For the full breakdown of features, pricing tiers, and real workflow examples, see our [Make.com review](/reviews/make-review).

**Key Strengths:**

- **Visual workflow builder**: The canvas-based editor lets you see your entire automation flow, including branches and error paths
- **Operations-based pricing**: $9/month Core plan includes 10,000 operations — far more capacity than Zapier's equivalent tier
- **Complex workflow support**: Native branching, loops, iterators, and data transformation without workarounds

**Key Weaknesses:**

- **Fewer integrations**: 1,800+ apps versus Zapier's 5,000+ — niche tools may not be supported
- **Steeper initial learning curve**: The visual canvas is powerful but takes longer to learn than Zapier's linear editor
- **Debugging UX needs work**: Error messages can be cryptic, and tracing failures through complex scenarios requires patience

## Head-to-Head Comparison

### Features

Zapier's headline feature is **breadth** — 5,000+ integrations mean you can connect virtually any combination of tools. Make.com's headline feature is **depth** — the visual canvas, HTTP modules, and data transformation tools let you build automations that would be impossible or impractical in Zapier's linear editor.

For workflows that follow a simple "when X happens, do Y" pattern, both tools perform equally well. The gap appears when you need conditional logic, data manipulation, or multi-branch workflows.

### Ease of Use

Zapier wins on **initial simplicity**. You can create your first automation in under 5 minutes with no prior experience. The step-by-step editor guides you through trigger selection, action configuration, and testing.

Make.com wins on **ongoing usability**. Once you learn the canvas (give it a week), building and modifying complex workflows is faster and more intuitive than managing multiple interconnected Zaps. The visual layout means you can see your entire automation logic at a glance.

### Output Quality

Both platforms execute automations reliably. Where they differ is in **what you can build**. A workflow that requires three separate Zaps in Zapier — each passing data to the next — can often be built as a single scenario in Make.com. Fewer moving parts means fewer points of failure.

### Pricing

| Feature | Zapier | Make.com |
|---|---|---|
| Free Tier | 100 tasks/month, 5 Zaps | 1,000 ops/month, 2 scenarios |
| Starting Paid | $19.99/month (750 tasks) | $9/month (10,000 ops) |
| Best Plan for Solos | Professional $49.99/month | Core $9/month |
| Best Plan for Teams | Team $69.99/month | Teams $29/month |
| Enterprise | Custom pricing | Custom pricing |

The pricing gap is significant. **Make.com's $9/month Core plan includes 10,000 operations** — enough for most freelancers and small businesses. Zapier's equivalent capacity starts at $19.99/month with only 750 tasks, and scales to $49.99/month for the Professional tier.

### Integrations

Zapier is the clear winner here with **5,000+ integrations** versus Make.com's **1,800+**. For mainstream tools (Google Workspace, Slack, HubSpot, Shopify, etc.), both platforms have solid coverage. The gap shows with niche or newer tools — Zapier is more likely to have a pre-built integration.

Make.com partially compensates with its **HTTP module**, which lets you connect to any API manually. This requires more technical knowledge but means no app is truly unsupported.
